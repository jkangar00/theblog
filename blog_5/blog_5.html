Location

Neighborhood: Lone Mountain

Price

$5.25 + $1

Wait Time

2-3mins

Ambiance

It’s a small cafe very clean and quiet. Definitely like a hidden gem study cafe. They have minimal seating but enough space where it’s not claustrophobic. There’s seating where you can see outside, so you can bask in the sun while you enjoy your drink without too much disturbance.

Flavor

The drink has a floral fragrant like jasmine and the taste is not a typical matcha flavor. It taste more like a jasmine iced tea, especially the after taste. Not something I’ve had before nor am particularly fond of. Despite that, it gets better as you drink it. I think it’s an acquired taste. It’s watered down too and no strong oat flavor. Overall very unique matcha flavor. You’ll like it if you’re fond of jasmine tea or tea in general.

Texture

The texture is thin and smooth. It has a dry finish. There are no particles or granular texture which is a good sign. The tea used makes the drink very refreshing.

Final

Notes

The cafe is accessible and close to many stores where you can get another bite to eat. It is right behind campus as well. There were some artisan pastries available and a small selection of other drinks to get. Since its on a busy street it has a lot of outside noise but very good for an after school pick up. The color is a bit of an off green, muted and brown. It comes solid without a gradient and the cup is plastic but straw free. I personally find drinks to be better with a straw so this place may not be it for you if that’s a deal breaker. The matcha seemed to be authentically made and the customer service was quick, but nothing special.

If you’ve made it this far, go out and try some Matcha! (or don’t)
